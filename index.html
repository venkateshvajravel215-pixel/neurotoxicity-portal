What is Neurotoxicity?

Neurotoxicity occurs when exposure to natural or manmade toxic substances (neurotoxicants) alters the normal activity of the nervous system.

Key Biological Markers

  • Neuronal cell death (Apoptosis)
  • Synaptic dysfunction
  • Oxidative stress in glial cells

Zebrafish: The Living Biosensor

Zebrafish are essential in studying Neurotoxicity because their transparent embryos allow us to see brain development in real-time. When exposed to pollutants, we can monitor "biological alarms" before damage becomes permanent.

Fig 1: High-resolution zebrafish model for toxicity testing.

Genetic Blueprint Extraction

By using Gene Extraction techniques, scientists isolate the mRNA responsible for regrowing spinal cords. Since humans share 70% of their DNA with zebrafish, this research is a bridge to curing human paralysis.
